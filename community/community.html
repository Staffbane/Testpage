Introduction

The Webberbridge Sports Hall is a great facility for the local residents of the Webberbridge Village and local area



The village of Webberbridge has an active fundraising committee, Mrs Weatherington is the chair of the committee and she is proud of the contribution everyone has made raising money through all sorts of events over the years including tombolas, welly wanging competitions, tugs of war. Recently it raised money to convert an old barn gifted to the village by a retired farmer David Plhough-Farrows into a multipurpose sports hall.

David lived in Webberbridge al his life and played cricket, football and dart for the village team and was the inaugural winner of the Webberbridge Bog-Snorkelling challenge which is run according to the Irish Bog-Snorkelling Association Rules.

The hall has markings for netball, hockey, tennis, badminton, 5 a-side football but also has nets available for games such as tennis and badminton, cricket.



In addition to the indoor activities the hall is the base for a number of other sports clubs including Golf, Bog Snorkelling, The hall is also suitable for a range of group activities such as dance or fitness classes and has even been used for childrenâ€™s parties.

Membership

Whilst the Sports Hall is open to the public becomming a member has its advantages. Membership is dependent on the following criteria

  1. You live in the Parish of Webberbridge
  2. You are a member of a club which uses the Webberbridge Sports Hall
  3. You have family connection with someone who meets criteria 1 or 2
  4. You have paid in full to use the Webberbridge Sports Halls at least 3 times in the past 3 months
